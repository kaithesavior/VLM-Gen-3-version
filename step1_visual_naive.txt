────────────────────────────────
STAGE-1 OBJECTIVE (NAIVE, SINGLE-FOCUS)
────────────────────────────────
Your task is NOT to infer smells.

Your task is to extract a simplified semantic summary of the video
by identifying the MOST SALIENT visual entity or action and tracking it
over the video timeline.

This baseline assumes that a single dominant object or action
captures the core semantic content of the video.

The goal is to produce a minimal, coarse-grained representation
without detailed contextual reasoning or fine-grained state modeling.

────────────────────────────────
ENTITY SELECTION STRATEGY (CRITICAL)
────────────────────────────────
You MUST select ONE primary semantic focus for the entire video.

The primary focus may be:
- the most visually salient object, OR
- the most frequently occurring object, OR
- the most prominent action repeatedly observed.

Selection criteria may include (use your best judgment):
- visual prominence (size, centrality, contrast),
- frequency of appearance,
- repeated interaction by an actor.

Once selected:
- ALL other objects, actions, and actors MUST be ignored.

────────────────────────────────
TEMPORAL ASSUMPTION (SIMPLIFIED)
────────────────────────────────
This baseline treats the video as a largely uniform semantic segment.

You MAY:
- represent the entire video as a single continuous interval, OR
- split into a small number of coarse intervals if a major change
  in the selected entity is visually obvious.

Fine-grained temporal segmentation is NOT required.

────────────────────────────────
VISUAL STATE REPRESENTATION (COARSE)
────────────────────────────────
For the selected primary entity, describe its state using
high-level, generic descriptors.

Allowed visual_state descriptions include:
- general appearance (present, active, visible),
- coarse interaction (being used, being handled),
- broad condition (unchanged, active, idle).

Do NOT model:
- detailed physical state transitions,
- exposure vs enclosure,
- material transformations,
- subtle surface or thermal cues.

This baseline intentionally avoids fine-grained state distinctions.

────────────────────────────────
STATE CHANGE DETECTION (MINIMAL)
────────────────────────────────
A state_change may be recorded ONLY if:
- there is an obvious, large-scale change involving the selected entity
  (e.g., object appears vs disappears; action starts vs ends).

Minor changes, repeated motions, or gradual transitions
SHOULD be ignored.

────────────────────────────────
INTERVAL REPRESENTATION RULES
────────────────────────────────
- Prefer ONE long interval covering most or all of the video.
- Use additional intervals only if the primary entity
  clearly changes in a major way.

Avoid detailed interval segmentation.

────────────────────────────────
RATIONALE REQUIREMENT
────────────────────────────────
Each interval MUST include a rationale explaining:
1) Why this entity was selected as the primary focus.
2) What is generally observable about it during this interval.

Rationales should be simple and descriptive.
They do NOT need to justify relevance to any downstream task.

────────────────────────────────
OUTPUT FORMAT
────────────────────────────────
You MUST output two components:

A. visual_timeline (PRIMARY OUTPUT)

A minimal list containing ONLY the selected primary entity.

Each entry MUST contain:
- entity_type: "object" | "action"
- entity_name: concise noun or verb phrase
- time: continuous interval (e.g., "0.0s - 12.0s")
- visual_state: high-level description
- state_change: true | false
- rationale: explanation for selection and interval division

B. frame_log
- One entry roughly every 1.0 second (MANDATORY).
- Content can be repetitive (e.g., "Main object visible").

{extra_requirements}
